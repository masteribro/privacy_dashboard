# Privacy Dashboard - Feature Guide

## Quick Start for New Users

Welcome to the enhanced Privacy Dashboard! Here's what's new and how to use it.

---

## 1. Data Requests (Subject Access Requests)

**What it is:** The legal right to request a copy of all data organizations hold about you (GDPR Article 15)

**How to use it:**
1. Click **"More → Data Requests"** in the navigation
2. Click **"New Request"** button
3. Fill in the request description
4. Choose your preferred format (JSON or CSV)
5. Agree to the confirmation
6. Submit

**What you'll get:**
- All your personal data from connected organizations
- Data sources and dates
- Consent records
- Your preferences and settings

**Timeline:** Requests are typically fulfilled within 30 days

---

## 2. CCPA Settings (for California Residents)

**What it is:** California Consumer Privacy Act rights to opt out of data sales and sharing

**How to use it:**
1. Click **"More → CCPA Settings"** in the navigation
2. Choose which opt-out type:
   - **Data Sales**: Opt out of selling your personal information
   - **Data Sharing**: Opt out of sharing with service providers
   - **Targeted Advertising**: Opt out of targeted ad campaigns
3. Select the organization
4. (Optional) Provide a reason
5. Click "Opt Out"

**Revoking opt-outs:**
1. Go to CCPA Settings
2. Click "Opt Back In" next to the opt-out you want to revoke

**Your rights:**
- Right to Know what data is collected
- Right to Delete personal data
- Right to Opt-Out of sales/sharing
- Right to Correct inaccurate data
- Right to Non-Discrimination

---

## 3. Audit Logs (Activity Tracking)

**What it is:** A complete record of everything you do in PrivacyFirst, for security and transparency

**How to use it:**
1. Click **"More → Audit Logs"** in the navigation
2. View your activity timeline
3. See what action was taken, when, and from which IP address
4. Check if it succeeded or failed

**What gets logged:**
- Viewing data items
- Exporting personal data
- Withdrawing consents
- Submitting data requests
- CCPA opt-outs
- Login activity
- Any failed actions

**Retention:** 
- Last 100 activities visible in UI
- Full archive maintained for 7 years for compliance

---

## 4. Data Flow Visualization

**What it is:** A visual representation of how your data moves through the ecosystem

**How to use it:**
1. Click **"More → Data Flow"** in the navigation
2. View the SVG diagram showing:
   - How you interact with PrivacyFirst
   - How data flows from organizations to the dashboard
   - Data sensitivity levels
3. Read privacy recommendations
4. Take quick actions from the action buttons

**Key insights:**
- **High sensitivity**: Financial, health, biometric data
- **Medium sensitivity**: Identity, preferences, contact info
- **Low sensitivity**: General website activity

## 7. Workflow Examples

### Scenario 1: You want a copy of all your data
1. Go to **Data Requests**
2. Click **New Request**
3. Describe what data you need
4. Choose JSON or CSV format
5. Submit
6. Wait for completion (typically 30 days)
7. Download your data

### Scenario 2: You're a California resident and want to opt out
1. Go to **CCPA Settings**
2. Choose "Opt-Out of Data Sales"
3. Select "SimulatedShop"
4. Click "Opt Out"
5. Confirmation appears - you've successfully opted out
6. Your preference is recorded in audit logs

### Scenario 3: You want to check what you've done
1. Go to **Audit Logs**
2. Scroll through your activity
3. See timestamps, actions, and statuses
4. Check for any suspicious activity
5. IP addresses shown for security verification

### Scenario 4: You want to understand your data
1. Go to **My Data**
2. See summary of organizations and data items
3. Click **Data Flow** to see visualization
4. Read privacy recommendations
5. Use Quick Actions to manage consents or export data

## 10. FAQs

**Q: How long does a Subject Access Request take?**
A: Typically 30 days. Complex requests may take up to 90 days. You'll be notified when it's ready.

**Q: Is there a cost for data requests?**
A: No, requests are free. Multiple copies may have a small charge.

**Q: Can I opt out retroactively?**
A: Opt-outs apply going forward. For historical data, file a deletion request.

**Q: How long are audit logs kept?**
A: Full archive for 7 years. Latest 100 activities visible in the dashboard.

**Q: What if I don't get a response to my SAR?**
A: If we don't respond within the timeframe, lodge a complaint with your Data Protection Authority:
- EU/EEA: Your national DPA
- UK: ICO (ico.org.uk)
- California: CPPA

**Q: Can I download my data in a specific format?**
A: Yes, we support JSON and CSV formats. Let us know your preference when submitting.

**Q: How do I know if my opt-out worked?**
A: Check your audit logs - your opt-out action will be recorded as successful. Organizations are notified of your preference.

## 12. Glossary

- **SAR**: Subject Access Request - your right to get a copy of your data
- **DPO**: Data Protection Officer - person responsible for privacy
- **GDPR**: General Data Protection Regulation (EU privacy law)
- **CCPA**: California Consumer Privacy Act (California privacy law)
- **RLS**: Row Level Security - database access control
- **Audit Log**: Record of who did what and when
- **Data Portability**: Right to get your data in a portable format
- **Consent**: Permission to use your data for a specific purpose
- **Data Subject**: You (the person whose data is being processed)
- **Data Controller**: Organization that decides how data is used

## 13. Troubleshooting

**My audit logs show failed actions:**
- Check your internet connection
- Try the action again
- Contact support if it persists

**I can't download my SAR data:**
- Make sure the request is marked "Completed"
- Try a different browser
- Contact the DPO if the link is broken

**CCPA opt-out not working:**
- Confirm you selected an organization
- Check audit logs to see if it was recorded
- Try opting in and then out again

**Navigation menu not showing all options:**
- Refresh the page
- Try logging out and back in
- Clear browser cache
- Use a different browser
